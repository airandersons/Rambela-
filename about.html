About Rwambela Drumline

Transforming lives through the power of rhythm, education, and community in Uganda since 2015

Our Journey

From Busabala to Beyond

Rwambela Drumline was founded in 2015 in the heart of Busabala, Kampala, by a group of musicians who saw the transformative power of drumming in their own lives and wanted to share it with vulnerable youth in their community.

What began as informal drumming sessions for street children has grown into a comprehensive program that provides not only musical training but also education, shelter, and life skills to youth who would otherwise have limited opportunities.

The name "Rwambela" comes from a local term meaning "to uplift" or "to elevate," which perfectly captures our mission of uplifting young people through music and community.

Our Mission

To transform the lives of vulnerable youth in Uganda through drumming, education, and community support, creating pathways to sustainable livelihoods and positive community impact.

Our Vision

A Uganda where every young person has access to education, artistic expression, and the opportunity to reach their full potential, regardless of their background or circumstances.

Milestones

Our Journey Through Time

Key moments in Rwambela's development

2015 - The Beginning

Rwambela Drumline is founded by Moses Kato and three fellow musicians in Busabala, Kampala. They begin informal drumming lessons for street children in the community.

2016 - First Formal Program

With donations from local businesses, Rwambela establishes its first structured program with 12 youth members. The group performs at its first wedding event.

2017 - Education Initiative

Rwambela begins using performance earnings to pay school fees for members. Partnership with local schools established to support educational needs.

2019 - Shelter Program

With growing support, Rwambela rents a house to provide shelter for its most vulnerable members. The dormitory can accommodate 15 youth at full capacity.

2020 - COVID-19 Response

Despite event cancellations during the pandemic, Rwambela pivots to online performances and receives international donations that help sustain the program.

2022 - Expansion

Rwambela expands to include vocational training in sound engineering and instrument repair. The drumline now performs at major corporate events across Kampala.

2023 - Present Day

Rwambela now supports 78 youth in education/training, provides 365 daily meals, and houses 32 members. The drumline has become a respected cultural institution in Uganda.

How We Work

Our Holistic Approach

Music Training

Daily drumming lessons, performance opportunities, and music theory education for all members.

Education Support

School fee payments, tutoring, vocational training, and scholarship opportunities for members.

Shelter & Meals

Safe dormitory housing and nutritious daily meals for our residential members.

Counseling & Mentorship

Regular counseling sessions and one-on-one mentorship with trained staff and volunteers.

Career Development

Job skills training, internship placements, and assistance with employment opportunities.

Community Reintegration

Support for family reunification and community integration when appropriate for each youth.
